Challenge 5: Accessibility Concerns

Challenge 2: Building a Chessboard presents an accessibility concern because it was built with an HTML table. While header and data cells can be marked to make their relationship more visible for screen readers, a screen reader typically reads cells from left to right, top to bottom, which may not be the correct content heirarchy. More complex tables with nested elements can make content very complex and hard to understand when being read by a screen reader.
